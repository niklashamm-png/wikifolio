Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is STRC?

STRC (Strategy Shares, ticker: STRC) is a preferred share issued by Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy). It pays regular dividends and provides indirect exposure to Bitcoin through the company's treasury holdings. It is designed to offer a more stable, income-generating alternative to holding MSTR common stock, while retaining upside participation if the Bitcoin price rises. In this portfolio, STRC serves as the defensive base – lower volatility, dividend cash flow, downside buffer.

What is MSTR?

MSTR is the stock ticker for MicroStrategy (now Strategy), a publicly listed company that has converted its treasury reserves almost entirely into Bitcoin. Because MSTR trades on equities markets and employs leverage (debt-financed Bitcoin purchases), it typically exhibits higher beta than Bitcoin itself – meaning it rises more in bull markets and falls more in bear markets. In this portfolio, MSTR is the higher-beta satellite whose weight increases when the Rainbow Chart signals undervaluation.

What is the Bitcoin Rainbow Chart?

The Bitcoin Rainbow Chart applies a logarithmic regression to Bitcoin's historical price data to create a slowly moving baseline. A set of color bands (from Purple at the bottom to Red at the top) is then overlaid to indicate whether Bitcoin is trading in an undervalued, fair-value, or overvalued zone relative to its long-term trend. Because Bitcoin's adoption follows an S-curve, a logarithmic scale captures this growth far better than a linear one. The zones update slowly – daily changes are minimal – making it ideal for a low-turnover, monthly rebalancing strategy.

How often is the portfolio rebalanced?

Rebalancing is signal-driven, not calendar-driven. The Rainbow Chart moves slowly (it is a logarithmic regression), so zone changes occur at most a few times per year under normal market conditions. A rebalance is executed only when the current Bitcoin price crosses into a new zone. This approach keeps transaction costs very low and avoids over-trading. In practice, rebalances occur roughly monthly or less frequently.

What are the key risks?

The following risks are material and must be understood before investing:

  • Concentration risk: The portfolio holds only two instruments (MSTR and STRC), both highly correlated to Bitcoin. Diversification across asset classes is minimal.
  • Bitcoin volatility: Both MSTR and STRC are directly or indirectly tied to Bitcoin, which can drop 50–80% in bear markets.
  • MSTR leverage risk: MSTR uses debt to purchase Bitcoin. In severe downturns, this leverage can amplify losses.
  • Model risk: The Rainbow Chart is a heuristic tool, not a guarantee. The model may miss turning points or trigger late rebalances.
  • Liquidity risk: wikifolio certificates may have limited liquidity in extreme market conditions.
  • Total loss risk: An investment in this wikifolio certificate can result in a total loss of the invested capital.
